COMMON STYLES

Common Hot Yoga styles found around you will be Hatha, Vinyasa and Yin.

Hatha

The Hatha style of yoga is typically taught using bikrama styled yoga. Bikrama styled yoga is meant for all levels of practioners. The class is typically 2 breathing excercises with 26 postures that will stay the same relatively depending on your teacher. Tempuratures range depending on studios between 100°F to 108°F with humdity of around 40%. This class is a great class to start your yoga practice with if you are a beginner.

Vinyasa

The Vinyasa style of yoga is more of a strength building yoga. Classes run at a faster pace because you flow from one pose direcctly into the next pose with your breathe. Most vinyasa classes are meant for all practioners but there are most advanced series. Tempuratures range from around 95°F give or take 5°F with humidity around 40%. This can be a be taken as a beginner but because of the faster pace it is recommended to have practiced yoga before.

Yin

The Yin style of yoga is a restorative flow. It is different from the other 2 styles because you relax into the postures without trying to use any muscles. The reason for this is because you are trying to stretch connective tissues. Postures are also held for longer periods of time that can range up to 8-10 minutes. Yin yoga is held at room tempurature. This is a great class to start with as a beginner and is meant for all practioners.
